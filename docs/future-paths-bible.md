# Future Paths Bible v1

## Vision

Future Paths is an identity exploration platform.

It helps people understand:

* Who they are becoming
* Who they are today
* Who they could have become

The product is built around decisions, outcomes, and identity evolution.

Future Paths does not tell users what to do.

It helps them understand how different choices shape different versions of themselves.

---

# Core Philosophy

People are not defined by single decisions.

People are shaped by patterns.

Identity is not static.

Identity is continuously emerging.

Future Paths exists to reveal those emerging patterns.

---

# The Three Core Questions

Every feature must strengthen at least one of these:

## 1. Who Am I Becoming?

Explored through:

* Moments
* Future Selves
* Identity Updates
* Theme Momentum

---

## 2. Who Am I Today?

Explored through:

* Current Self
* Identity Prompts
* Check-Ins
* Contradictions

---

## 3. Who Could I Have Become?

Explored through:

* Reflections
* Alternate Selves
* Unchosen Paths

---

# Core Concepts

## Moments

A Moment is a meaningful decision, uncertainty, crossroads, or situation.

Examples:

* Should I move cities?
* Should I start the business?
* Should I reconnect with them?
* Should I leave my job?

Moments are the foundation of the system.

---

## Paths

Each Moment generates multiple possible paths.

A path includes:

* Description
* Benefits
* Consequences
* Future Shift

Paths are possibilities, not recommendations.

Future Paths never ranks paths as correct or incorrect.

---

## Check-Ins

Check-Ins connect decisions to reality.

Moments capture intention.

Check-Ins capture outcomes.

Reality carries more weight than prediction.

---

## Future Selves

Future Selves are identity trajectories.

They are not fixed destinies.

They emerge from repeated patterns.

Examples:

* The Connector
* The Explorer
* The Builder
* The Creator
* The Guardian
* The Learner
* The Pioneer
* The Visionary
* The Mentor
* The Peacemaker
* The Adventurer
* The Craftsman

Users may have multiple Future Selves at once.

Each future has momentum.

Momentum rises and falls over time.

---

## Current Self

Current Self is the platform's best understanding of who the user is today.

It is:

* Dynamic
* Evidence-based
* Continuously updated

Current Self should never be presented as absolute truth.

Use language such as:

"You currently tend to..."

Avoid:

"You are..."

---

## Reflections

Reflections explore unchosen paths.

They answer:

"What might have happened if I chose differently?"

Reflections are not regret engines.

They are perspective engines.

---

## Alternate Selves

Alternate Selves emerge from repeated unchosen paths.

They represent identities that could have developed but did not.

Examples:

* The Adventurer
* The Entrepreneur
* The Artist

Alternate Selves help users understand missed trajectories.

---

## Timeline

The Timeline is the user's identity history.

Everything becomes part of the Timeline:

* Moments
* Path Selections
* Check-Ins
* Future Emergence
* Reflections
* Identity Updates

The Timeline answers:

"How did I become this version of myself?"

---

# Identity Themes

Future Paths tracks recurring themes.

Examples:

* Connection
* Independence
* Curiosity
* Stability
* Creativity
* Growth
* Belonging
* Leadership
* Reflection
* Courage

Themes are the hidden layer beneath Future Selves.

Themes influence identity trajectories.

---

# Identity Updates

Identity Updates occur when meaningful changes happen.

Examples:

* A future emerges
* A future fades
* A contradiction appears
* A recurring pattern strengthens

Identity Updates should be insightful, not noisy.

---

# Product Principles

Future Paths should feel:

* Reflective
* Calm
* Intelligent
* Human
* Premium

Avoid:

* Gamification
* Streaks
* Badges
* Leaderboards
* Productivity culture

---

# AI Principles

AI should:

* Explore possibilities
* Reveal patterns
* Encourage reflection

AI should never:

* Give life advice
* Judge users
* Diagnose users
* Predict certainty

Future Paths reveals trajectories, not destinies.

---

# Success

A successful user can:

* Capture meaningful decisions
* Revisit real outcomes
* Understand recurring patterns
* Explore future identities
* Reflect on alternate identities
* Build a living map of who they were, who they are, and who they are becoming

That is Future Paths.
